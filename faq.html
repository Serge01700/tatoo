Your Questions - FAQ

How long does it take to get a tattoo?

The time it takes to complete a tattoo depends on its size, complexity and the area of the body involved. On average, a small tattoo can take from 1 to 2 hours, while a larger tattoo may require several sessions.

Does the tattoo hurt?

The sensation of pain varies from person to person, but most people describe a tingling sensation or discomfort during the process. The level of pain also depends on the area of the body where the tattoo is performed.

How to choose the right tattoo artist?

Take the time to research and consult the portfolios of different tattoo artists. Make sure they have experience in the style of tattooing you're looking for. Read reviews from previous customers to get an idea of their reputation.

What post-tattoo care is recommended?

After a tattoo has been applied, it's essential to follow the care instructions provided by the tattoo artist. This may include regular cleaning, application of a healing ointment, and avoidance of prolonged exposure to the sun.

How long does it take to heal a tattoo?

Tattoo healing can take from a few weeks to a few months. The length of time depends on the size of the tattoo, its location and how quickly your skin heals.

Can I bring in a custom tattoo idea?

Yes, you can! Many tattoo artists accept personalized ideas. Bring visual references or sketches to discuss your vision with the tattoo artist and collaborate on a unique design.

What are the risks associated with tattoos?

Tattoos carry certain risks, such as infections and allergic reactions. By following care instructions and choosing a reputable professional, you can reduce these risks.

Do all tattoos require retouching?

Touch-ups are not always necessary, but they may be recommended to improve the color or detail of the tattoo. It depends on the type of tattoo and how your skin reacts.

Can I get a tattoo if I'm pregnant or breast-feeding?

Tattooing is generally not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding, due to the potential risks involved. Consult your health care professional before making any decisions.

How can I make an appointment with a tattoo artist?

Contact the tattooist of your choice directly by phone, email or via their website. Most tattoo artists require a deposit to reserve a date.
